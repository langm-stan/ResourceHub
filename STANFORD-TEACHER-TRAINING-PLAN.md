# Teacher Training Plan (revised July 10, 2026)

Personal finance training for high school teachers at Stanford, July 13-17.
The week was resequenced after the July 3 draft: Gambling vs. Investing now
runs Wednesday afternoon (Lecture 3B), and one Thursday-morning hour
(Lecture 4A) carries tax efficiency, employer benefits, and retirement,
with basic personal-tax mechanics compressed into its on-ramp. Housing,
cars, and durable goods moved to Thursday afternoon. The old two-session
plan below the divider is kept for reference; its 3B outline was the seed
for 4A.

Slide decks in `../Teacher Training/`, one file per session, all built from
the 2A template. NOTE the legacy filenames from the July 3 round are
misleading: `PFinTrainingLectureTaxShelter.pptx` contains the old "Personal
Taxes and Housing" deck and `PFinTrainingLectureHousing.pptx` contains the
old "Tax Efficiency" deck. The current Thursday-morning deck is
`PFinTrainingLecture4A.pptx` (rebuilt July 10; scratch generator kept in the
session scratchpad, charts native).

Every tool supports `?embed=1` for slides/LMS and hover-to-download PNG on
every chart.

The act transitions are consequences: the credit score prices the car loan
(Act II) and reappears pricing the mortgage (slide 19); the car payment
from Act II shrinks the Act III house on the debts chart (slide 18, "the
car is parked in the house budget"); Act IV's itemize test uses the
morning's standard deduction.

Numbers cross-checked July 10: laptop $2,000 / $90 x 36 = 34.6% (matches
Econ 43 L5); car $40,000 at 7%/60mo = $792.05 ($7,523 interest), dealer
8.5% = $820.66 ($9,240), difference $1,717; 84-month = $603.71, $10,711
interest, underwater years 1-3 (KBB depreciation: ~20% year one, ~60% by
five); Experian Q4 2025: average new-car term 68.9 months, 31.8% at 73+
months; AAA 2025: $11,577/year (~$965/month); mortgage $336,000 at 6.4% =
$2,101.70, interest $420,612, outlay $840,612, crossover year 20, year-1
split $21,393/$3,827, year-30 split $853/$24,367; afford ceiling $368,186
at $0-600 debts, $331,367 at $900, $284,029 at $1,200 (kink $667; $300 of
car past the kink = ~$47,000 of house); credit tiers 6.4/6.7/7.5% =
$2,102/$2,168/$2,349 ($248/mo, $89,158); 15v30 at $2,500/mo: $354,252 vs.
$455,825 (Econ 43 L7); itemize $26,013 vs. $32,200/$16,100, single saves
$2,181; MC Hammer $33M/1991, $30M mansion, sold $5M, $70M to -$13M (Econ
43 L7).

Added July 11 (both verified):
- Used vs. new (slide 9): three-year-old $40,000 car ~ $23,000 (KBB curve);
  Experian Q4 2025 average APRs 6.37% new / 11.26% used; used at 11%/60mo =
  $500.08/mo, $7,005 interest (vs. $792.05 / $7,523 new at 7%): near-parity
  interest, the bigger loan cancels the better rate; $792 against the used
  loan pays it off in 34 months.
- Year-one rent-vs-own test (slide 28): pure cost $32,313 ($21,393 interest
  + $4,620 property tax + $2,100 insurance + $4,200 upkeep at the 1% rule)
  = $2,693/mo; down-payment opportunity cost $84,000 x 7% = $490/mo;
  principal credit $319/mo; net ~$2,860/mo = the break-even rent the room
  is asked to beat. Closing costs excluded (they raise the break-even).

## Numbers cross-checked (July 2026)

- Take-home hook (matches Station 1 defaults): $60,000 single filer, 2026
  federal + FICA: $5,020 income tax ($1,240 + $3,780), $4,590 FICA,
  take-home $50,390; effective 8.4% vs. 12% marginal; $2,000 raise nets
  +$1,607.
- Taxed twice vs. once (annual version, matches Station 2 defaults):
  $3,600/yr of earnings at 6%/40 yrs, 30% tax: $251,071 vs. $390,000
  (gap $138,929); Roth identical at equal rates; chart endpoints verified.
  (The one-deposit Lusardi original, $13,065 vs. $25,920, is no longer on
  slides.)
- Station 3 opening state: 3% of $50,000 collects $750 of $1,500;
  FV($750, 7%, 40) = $149,726.
- Rate-not-age (Station 4 model, start 25, 7%/3.5%, 30-yr pile): save
  10% -> optional at 63; 15% -> 57; 25% -> 49; 50% -> 38.
- Paycheck: $80k CA earner, $5,000 into 401(k), take-home falls $3,477
  (engine-verified, 2026 tables).
- 2026 limits: 401(k) $24,500, IRA $7,500 (IRS Notice 2025-67); HSA
  $4,400 / $8,750 (Rev. Proc. 2025-19).
- Match: 50 cents per dollar on first 6% is the most common formula
  (Vanguard, How America Saves); Financial Engines (2015): 1 in 4 leave
  match, avg $1,336/yr, ~$24B/yr.
- Match doubling: $7,000/yr start-of-year, 8%, 40 yrs: $1.96M -> $3.92M.
- Two-step: $70k x 30 yrs at 3.5% -> $1,287,443; 35 yrs at 7% -> $9,313/yr.
- Waiting ($1.4M by 65 at 7%): 40 yrs $7,013, 35 yrs $10,128, 25 yrs
  $22,135 (ages 25/30/40 on the slide, consistent with retiring at 65).
- Returns disappoint: 5% then 1.5%: $841,153 -> ~$35,025/yr.
- Freedom (7% real, 25x spending): 10% -> ~41 yrs, 15% -> ~35, 25% -> ~27,
  50% -> <15.
- Auto-enrollment: participation 37% -> 86% (Madrian & Shea 2001, QJE).
- Social Security replaces ~40% of the average earner's wage (SSA).
- Ben's brackets example: $65,000 -> $9,214 (2025 brackets, no deductions),
  effective ~14%.

Annual refresh list: slide 14 limits (401k/IRA), slide 16 (HSA), slide 8
