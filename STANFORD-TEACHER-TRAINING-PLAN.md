# Two-Hour Teacher Training Plan (revised order)

Personal finance training for high school teachers at Stanford, two 1-hour
sessions. Revised July 2026: **personal taxes and housing now run first**, and
tax efficiency, employer benefits, and retirement close the day. The morning
teaches what the system takes; the afternoon teaches the legal ways to keep
more, ending on the question students actually care about: when can you stop
working?

Slide decks (built from the 2A template, one file per session):
`../Teacher Training/PFinTrainingLecture3A.pptx` and `3B.pptx`. Chart PNGs and
their matplotlib generator: `../Teacher Training/Lecture3-Charts/`. Content
draws on Econ 43 Lectures 12 (Personal Taxes), 7 (Housing), 13 (Workplace
Saving), and 14 (Planning for Retirement).

Every tool below supports `?embed=1` for slides/LMS and hover-to-download PNG
on every chart (instructor bar at the bottom of each lesson page).

Dense by design (Matt prefers more information over less). Pressure valves if
time runs short: Frank (slide 27), points/refinancing (slide 31), and doors

default household that cannot afford the default house; fair-vs-excellent
credit worth $248/month; the married couple whose $26,013 of home deductions
lose to the $32,200 standard deduction (folk-wisdom bust), flipped by one
filing-status toggle.

**Arc: the same code, used in your favor, ending on freedom.**

| Time | Segment | Tool on screen |

The finale is the interactive beat: teachers open the freedom lesson on their
own devices, play the four lives (NBA rookie, teacher, engineer, barista: the
rookie out-earns the barista 20-to-1 and reaches freedom LAST), then run the
$500-less-spending experiment and the match toggle. It is the morning's MC
Hammer story replayed as a game students control.

## How the day answers the participants' concerns

Relevance (every segment starts from a number students will face; the finale
is about freedom, not old age); teacher knowledge gaps in taxes (the full
mechanics run first, with the tools doing the arithmetic); equity (rent vs.
buy argued honestly, down payment named as the real barrier); math anxiety
(predict-then-reveal, tools compute, "The math" tabs for derivations);
misinformation (raise myth, "buy a house for the tax break," "renting is
throwing money away," each killed with live arithmetic); curriculum resources
(embed codes and PNG exports on every chart, named on the closing slide).
